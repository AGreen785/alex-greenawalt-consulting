The Problem I Solve

The greatest driver
of poor health outcomes
is not clinical failure.

It is coordination failure.

The systems designed to serve the same person — healthcare, housing, behavioral health, community organizations — don't talk to each other, don't trust each other, and don't share accountability.

The people caught between those systems bear the cost — in their bodies, their stability, and their lives.

Coordination is not an event.
It is a living system.

Real coordination infrastructure has to be tended, evaluated, and renewed. The data gets reviewed. The gaps get addressed. The partners stay at the table — not because of a contract deadline, but because the infrastructure makes showing up worth it. That is the continuum. That is the standard I hold every engagement to. What was once fringe is now embedded in state Medicaid strategy and payment systems — CMS has created reimbursable SDOH pathways through Z-code screening and expanded Section 1115 waiver demonstrations, with 18 HRSN waivers approved and additional states in active negotiation. Health systems are accelerating community partnerships to meet payer expectations and value-based contract requirements.

Policy momentum and operational readiness are two different things — fewer than a third of physician practices screen for the most common social risk factors.

The contracts are being awarded.  The timelines are live.  Most organizations still don't know how to build this.

That infrastructure is what I build. And I know exactly where to begin.

Project 01

Collective Impact Backbone +
Enterprise Data Infrastructure

Pathways Community Center · York County, SC

20+ Organizations $850K Budget 2021–2024
The Problem

20+ health and human service agencies were serving the same clients with no shared data, no coordinated entry, and no mechanism to close the loop on a completed referral. The result: duplicated services, wasted resources, and no accountability for outcomes across the network.

Methodology & Approach

Applied the Collective Impact (FSG) framework to design and operate a Community Care Hub in York County — modeled on CMS Accountable Health Communities and Pathways Community Hub frameworks — aligning 20+ agencies under unified intake and coordinated service delivery. Co-designed the hub's partner payment framework, including a revenue-sharing model that returned a portion of outcomes-based reimbursement to referring CBOs; network lead and Medicaid billing functions were administered by the federal community action agency. Implemented Unite Us as the closed-loop referral infrastructure, aligned to CMS Community Health Integration standards. Built a Results-Based Accountability measurement system with monthly QI cycles. Developed an implementation playbook presented at national health equity conference (Cencora/AmerisourceBergen).

Project 02

Financial Navigation Program +
Hybrid Reimbursement Sustainability Model

Carolina Blood & Cancer Care Associates · SC

1,000+ Cancer Patients DBA Dissertation Research
The Problem

A community oncology practice serving 1,000+ cancer patients was delivering measurable SDOH care navigation with no infrastructure connecting those services to clinical workflows, payer systems, or a sustainable reimbursement model. Without that bridge, the program faced elimination. The gap wasn't clinical. It was infrastructure.

Methodology & Approach

Mapped the full care navigation workflow end-to-end — from SDOH screening and Z-code documentation to G-code billing and community referral closure — identifying where coordination broke down and where value was being lost. Applied CMS Community Health Integration and Collective Impact principles to bridge clinical and community workflows across organizational boundaries. Applied financial modeling across fee-for-service, value-based care, and blended finance scenarios to design a sustainable hybrid reimbursement model. Used Lean process mapping to redesign workflows and optimize billing capture. Structured a pharmaceutical partnership strategy through the NOLA 501(c)(3) to close the remaining funding gap. The result: an implementation playbook for sustaining SDOH coordination infrastructure within community-based healthcare settings — directly applicable to Medicaid managed care and value-based care contracting.

Project 03

Housing Crisis Response System →
HUD-Aligned Continuum of Care

Catawba Area Coalition for the Homeless · Multi-County, SC

100+ Cross-Sector Partners Board Secretary
The Problem

A multi-county region with accelerating homelessness and growing street encampments had no functioning Continuum of Care — no coordinated entry, no HUD-aligned governance structure, and no infrastructure to access federal housing funds or sub-grant to CBO partners. Without it, communities couldn't move people off the streets and into stable housing at scale.

Methodology & Approach

Applied Collective Impact framework and national benchmarking (Houston "The Way Home," Charlotte-Mecklenburg Continuum of Care) to transform a dormant networking coalition into a HUD-aligned Continuum of Care — structured around National Alliance to End Homelessness standards and Housing First principles. Built governance infrastructure with designated sub-committees and coordinated entry protocols to enable street outreach, rapid rehousing, and systematic CBO sub-granting. Produced the region's first homelessness policy brief, establishing credibility with funders and policymakers, securing the coalition's first Executive Director hire, and positioning housing instability as a documented SDOH driver. The governance model became an implementation playbook replicated by coalitions across South Carolina.

Project 04

Statewide Opioid
Emergency Response System

Keystone Substance Abuse Services · Statewide SC

$5.7M Behavioral Health Portfolio 100+ Clinicians
The Problem

South Carolina's behavioral health and public health systems had no shared coordination infrastructure — no real-time data to track where crises were emerging, no cross-sector accountability, no common response protocol. Law enforcement, hospitals, behavioral health providers, and community organizations were each responding in isolation to the same crisis. People were dying in the gap.

Methodology & Approach

Applied the Strategic Prevention Framework to build a cross-sector behavioral health coordination system — aligning law enforcement, hospitals, behavioral health providers, and public health agencies around a shared real-time data infrastructure (ODMAP). Designed a three-tier communication and response network that converted live data into coordinated deployment within 48 hours. Built a scalable community behavior change program to extend the coordination infrastructure to the community level — reaching 3,000+ people and sustaining impact beyond the grant period.
